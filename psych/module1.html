What does emotion mean?

Us humans are built for survival, we want to belong and we thrive to become.

  Without emotion we have a lesser ability to communicate with each other. When we're sad, someone nearby might step over and ask what's wrong. When we're angry someone might be afraid to ask what's wrong. How might that work without our facial expressions for emotion? Some people have a high intillectual intelligence, however, intillectual intelligence isn't all thats important. Having a high emotional intelligence is also a very saught after skill.

  • Self-management
  • Self-awareness
  • Social-awareness
  • Empathy
  • Relationship management
  • Leading a diverse team
  • Leading team to finishing goals

  To have a higher emotional intelligence, one must better themselves at any of the above. Not mentioned above, you can also learn more about emotion itself (specifically the biology behind it) to also achieve a higher emotional intelligence.

  I'ts often thought that emotions are binary in that we think of them as either completely happy/sad or angry/neutral. This is not the case at all. There are dimensions to emotions for example, sometimes you might feel happy but also excited. Emotions live on a spectrum just like Plutchik's wheel of emotions (see above-right), they are constantly shifting based on the stimuli or thoughts that activate them.
