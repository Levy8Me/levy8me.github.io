Emotional Intelligence:

  People with high emotional intelligence are typically skilled in self-management, self-awareness, social-awareness, empathy, relationship management, leading a diverse team and leading a team to completion of goals. To have a higher emotional intelligence, one must better themselves in any of these areas. Not mentioned above, you can also learn more about emotion itself (specifically the biology behind it) to also achieve a higher emotional intelligence.

  I'ts often thought that emotions are binary in that we think of them as either completely happy/sad or angry/neutral. This is not the case at all. There are dimensions to emotions for example, sometimes you might feel happy but also excited. Emotions live on a spectrum just like Plutchik's wheel of emotions (see above-right), they are constantly shifting based on the stimuli or thoughts that activate them.

Definitions for Emotion:

  There are three different definitions of emotion. The first definition is the evolutionist definition which views emotion as discrete, natural responses that are biologically driven, and having specific facial expressions. The second is the constructivist definition which views emotion as a social construct, subject to culture, and does not consider animal research in its analysis. The third and final definition is the objectivist definition it looks at both human and animal emotions, using animal research to inform human research, and focusing mainly on objective properties.
